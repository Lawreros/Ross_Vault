###### Content:
Suppose $T \in \mathcal{L}(V)$. A [[subspace]] $U$ of $V$ is called **invariant** under $T$ if $u \in U$ implies $Tu \in U$

**Example**
Suppose $T \in \mathcal{L}(V)$. Each of the following subspaces of $V$ is invariant under $T$:
- {0}
- $V$
- null $T$
- range $T$
